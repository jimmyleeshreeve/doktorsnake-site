# Black Work Of Hell

<p class="text">Dire, desperate, dreadful... when the job seriously needs to be done.</p>

<img class="about-hero" src="/assets/img/satan-goat.jpg"
     alt="Satan Goat">

This hell-bane curse works on evil-doers, narcissists, troublemakers, those that exploit you, bad relationships partners - anybody that is a wrong-doer.

It will put a stop to their malign machinations. Can also be configured to push them to wake up - IF it's in them to do so (it might not be).

When the going gets tough, it's time to enlist the help of the demons in the nether regions of reality, whose attributes fit the person or persons you need to hit with a hex.

Ideal for this job are the lesser demons such as Bechaud, Guland, or Huictiigora. Which one depends on your case and who you are targeting. In this case, by the way, "lesser" means in rank, but more than capable of unleashing the required consequences.

If you've got a situation going on that needs rectifying, or a person that merits their just desserts, this working will do it.

It pulls no punches. It rides in like the cavalry to put things to rights. It will either wake them up to their wrongdoing or take them down.

> You are in safe hands. I do the “dirty work” of dealing with the specific demon that has the powers to fulfil your requirements.

## How it works

For this working, I use updated ritual formulas from the *Grimorium Verum*, an old book of magic from the 18th century, but purportedly originating from medieval times.

During my ceremony, I summon the required demon, the one best qualified to cause mayhem on your target, along with inscribing that demon's sigil (or name) on a clay tablet.

After intoning infernal incantations, I petition the denizen of the hell realm to grant your request. When done, we bury it, leaving it to do its baleful work.

Depending on the clemency of the weather, I perform this ritual work after the sun sets in the western skies, typically at a 5,000-year-old Neolithic round barrow in the wilds of the Suffolk - once a ritual site for the ancient Britons and ideally suited to the conjuring of demons.
