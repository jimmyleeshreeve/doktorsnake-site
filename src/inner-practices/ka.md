## What did the ancient Egyptians believe that truly makes a human being alive?

Long before modern psychology, neuroscience, or systems theory, they spoke of the Ka—the invisible vital essence that accompanies every person from birth, animates the body throughout life, and endures beyond death.

Whether understood as a religious idea, a living metaphor, or a profound model of consciousness, the Ka invites a simple but enduring question:

## What is it that has been alive within you all along?

This guided meditation draws inspiration from Ancient Egyptian thought and gently leads you into direct contemplation of that question.

Rather than asking you to believe anything, it offers space to become aware of something quieter than thought, deeper than personality, and older than the stories we tell ourselves.

Accompanied by an immersive soundtrack and atmospheric imagery, this is a reflective inner journey into one of humanity's oldest conceptions of life itself.

## Future meditations will explore:

The Ba — the unique personality and dreamer.  
The Ren — the hidden power of the true name.  
The Ib — the heart as the centre of wisdom and judgment.  
The Akh — the transformed and luminous self.

Together they form a contemplative journey through one of the world's most enduring spiritual traditions.
